### 1. Video Script 2.1: The Logical Structure of AD DS
**Estimated Duration:** 6 minutes
**Visual:** Instructor on camera.
**Audio:** "Welcome to Module 2. If you want to manage 1,000 Windows computers, you don't walk around to each one creating local user accounts. You use a centralized directory. In the Microsoft world, this is Active Directory Domain Services, or AD DS."
**Visual:** A pyramid diagram showing the AD DS hierarchy: Forest at the top, Trees below, Domains below Trees, and Organizational Units (OUs) at the bottom.
**[Alt-text: A hierarchical diagram. The top layer is a single triangle labeled 'Forest'. Below it are two triangles labeled 'Trees'. Below those are circles labeled 'Domains'. Inside the domains are small folders labeled 'Organizational Units (OUs)'.]**
**Audio:** "AD DS uses a strict logical structure. The highest level is the Forest, representing the entire organization. Inside the Forest are Trees, which share a contiguous namespace. Inside Trees are Domains, like `txwes.edu`. And inside Domains, we create Organizational Units, or OUs, to group our users and computers—like an OU for HR and an OU for IT. This structure dictates how permissions and Group Policies flow."
