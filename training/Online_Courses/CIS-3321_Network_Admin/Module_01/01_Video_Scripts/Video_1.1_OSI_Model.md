# Video Script 1.1: The OSI Model Demystified
**Course:** CIS 3321 - Network Administration (CompTIA Network+)
**Module:** 1 (Networking Fundamentals)
**Estimated Duration:** 6 minutes

---

## Script & Visual Directives

**[00:00 - 01:00] Introduction**
**Visual:** Instructor on camera.
**Audio:** "Welcome to CIS 3321, Network Administration. I'm Professor Nash. Today, we're tackling the foundational concept of all computer networking: The OSI Model. Whether you're troubleshooting a home Wi-Fi issue or managing a massive enterprise datacenter, the OSI model is the blueprint you'll rely on."

**[01:00 - 02:30] What is the OSI Model?**
**Visual:** Graphic appears on screen showing the 7 layers of the OSI model stacked from bottom to top (1. Physical, 2. Data Link, 3. Network, 4. Transport, 5. Session, 6. Presentation, 7. Application).
**[Alt-text: A vertical list of 7 rectangular blocks representing the OSI model. Bottom to top: Layer 1 is Physical, Layer 2 is Data Link, Layer 3 is Network, Layer 4 is Transport, Layer 5 is Session, Layer 6 is Presentation, Layer 7 is Application.]**
**Audio:** "The Open Systems Interconnection, or OSI model, breaks down network communication into seven distinct layers. Think of it like a manufacturing assembly line. Each layer has a very specific job and passes the product—your data—to the next layer."

**[02:30 - 04:30] The Upper Layers vs. Lower Layers**
**Visual:** The OSI graphic splits. Layers 5-7 are highlighted in blue (Software/Data). Layers 1-4 are highlighted in orange (Hardware/Transport).
**[Alt-text: The 7-layer OSI model graphic. The top three layers (Application, Presentation, Session) are highlighted in blue and labeled 'Software'. The bottom four layers (Transport, Network, Data Link, Physical) are highlighted in orange and labeled 'Hardware/Transport'.]**
**Audio:** "Layers 7, 6, and 5 deal with the software side. This is your web browser, encryption, and the session connections. Layers 4 through 1 are where the true networking magic happens. Layer 4 handles TCP and UDP transport. Layer 3 is the Network layer—this is where your IP addresses and Routers live. Layer 2 is the Data Link layer, home to MAC addresses and Switches. Finally, Layer 1 is the Physical layer—the actual cables and radio waves."

**[04:30 - 06:00] Mnemonic and Conclusion**
**Visual:** Text on screen: "Please Do Not Throw Sausage Pizza Away" aligning with the layers (Physical to Application).
**Audio:** "A great way to memorize this for the Network+ exam is the phrase: 'Please Do Not Throw Sausage Pizza Away.' Physical, Data Link, Network, Transport, Session, Presentation, Application. In our next video, we'll see how these layers form different network topologies. Thanks for watching, and I'll see you in the next burst."
