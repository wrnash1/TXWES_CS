### 1. Video Script 3.1: 802.11 Standards Breakdown
**Estimated Duration:** 6 minutes
**Visual:** Instructor on camera.
**Audio:** "Welcome to Module 3. We're cutting the cord. Wireless networking relies on the IEEE 802.11 family of standards. The Network+ exam expects you to know the differences in speeds, frequencies, and ranges of these standards."
**Visual:** A table comparing 802.11a, b, g, n, ac, and ax.
**[Alt-text: A table titled 'Wi-Fi Standards'. 802.11a: 5GHz, 54Mbps. 802.11b: 2.4GHz, 11Mbps. 802.11g: 2.4GHz, 54Mbps. 802.11n (Wi-Fi 4): 2.4/5GHz, up to 600Mbps. 802.11ac (Wi-Fi 5): 5GHz, Gbps speeds. 802.11ax (Wi-Fi 6): 2.4/5GHz, highest capacity.]**
**Audio:** "The 2.4 GHz band penetrates walls better but is crowded with Bluetooth and microwaves, causing interference. The 5 GHz band is much faster but has a shorter range. Modern standards like Wi-Fi 6 (802.11ax) use both bands simultaneously and introduce technologies like MU-MIMO to handle dozens of devices at once."
