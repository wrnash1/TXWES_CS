### 2. Video Script 4.2: Firewalls and IDS/IPS
**Estimated Duration:** 7 minutes
**Visual:** Network diagram showing the flow of traffic from the internet, through a firewall, through an IPS, to a switch.
**[Alt-text: A diagram showing traffic flowing from the Internet (Cloud icon) into a brick wall (Firewall). Allowed traffic passes through to a magnifying glass (IPS). If safe, it passes to the internal Switch.]**
**Audio:** "To defend our perimeter, we use Firewalls and Intrusion Prevention Systems (IPS). A standard firewall operates at Layer 3 and Layer 4 of the OSI model. It uses Access Control Lists (ACLs) to block traffic based on IP addresses and Port numbers. However, standard firewalls don't look inside the packet payload. That's the job of the IPS. An IPS operates at Layer 7. It inspects the actual data looking for known malware signatures or anomalous behavior. If it sees an attack, the IPS drops the packet immediately."
