# CIS 3321: Zero Textbook Cost (ZTC) OER Guide
**Target Certification:** CompTIA Network+ (N10-008)

This course uses a Zero Textbook Cost model. Instead of purchasing an expensive textbook, you will use the following free Open Educational Resources (OER) curated directly from industry professionals.

## Primary OER Textbook Replacements
You will rely heavily on the free video training course provided by Professor Messer, as well as official Cisco/CompTIA documentation.

### Module 1-4: Networking Fundamentals & Security
*   **Reading 1:** [Professor Messer's Free Network+ N10-008 Video Course](https://www.professormesser.com/network-plus/n10-008/n10-008-video/n10-008-training-course/)
    *   *Focus:* Watch Section 1.0 (Networking Fundamentals) and Section 4.0 (Network Security).
*   **Reading 2:** [Cisco Networking Academy - Introduction to Networks (Free Course)](https://skillsforall.com/)
    *   *Focus:* Read the modules regarding the OSI Model, TCP/IP, and basic routing.

### Module 5-8: Advanced Routing, Wireless, & Operations
*   **Reading 3:** [Professor Messer's Free Network+ N10-008 Video Course](https://www.professormesser.com/network-plus/n10-008/n10-008-video/n10-008-training-course/)
    *   *Focus:* Watch Section 3.0 (Network Operations) and Section 5.0 (Network Troubleshooting).
*   **Reading 4:** [Wi-Fi Alliance Security Standards (WPA3)](https://www.wi-fi.org/discover-wi-fi/security)

### Module 9-16: Subnetting, IPv6, & Final Prep
*   **Reading 5:** [Practical Networking - Free Subnetting Mastery Series](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLIFyRwBY_4bQUE4IB5c4VPRyDoLgOdExE)
    *   *Focus:* This free series replaces the need for any textbook chapters on IPv4 and IPv6 subnetting.
*   **Reading 6:** [Official CompTIA N10-008 Exam Objectives (PDF)](https://www.comptia.org/certifications/network)
    *   *Focus:* Print this free PDF and use it as your final checklist.

> **Instructor Note:** When the Blackboard Module asks you to read the "High-Yield Concepts", you should review those bullet points and then find the corresponding video or free article in the links above to read the deep-dive explanation.
