### 2. Video Script 1.2: Hypervisors and VirtualBox Setup
**Estimated Duration:** 7 minutes
**Visual:** Screencast of Oracle VirtualBox manager.
**[Alt-text: A screencast of the Oracle VM VirtualBox Manager window. The user clicks 'New', names the VM 'Ubuntu Server', and allocates 2048 MB of RAM.]**
**Audio:** "To practice safely, we will use a Hypervisor. A hypervisor allows us to run multiple virtual machines on a single physical computer. VirtualBox is a Type 2 hypervisor, meaning it runs on top of your existing host OS, like Windows 10 or macOS. Let's walk through creating your first virtual machine. Notice how we allocate a specific chunk of our host's RAM and CPU cores to the guest VM. This sandboxing ensures that if you accidentally destroy your Linux VM, your host computer remains perfectly safe."
