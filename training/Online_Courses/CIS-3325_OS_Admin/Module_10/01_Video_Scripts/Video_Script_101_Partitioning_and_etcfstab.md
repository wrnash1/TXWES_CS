**Video Script 10.1: Partitioning and /etc/fstab**
*Audio:* "When you plug a new hard drive into a Linux server, it doesn't automatically show up as a D: drive. You must partition it with `fdisk`, format it with a filesystem like `ext4`, and explicitly `mount` it to a directory. To make the mount survive a reboot, you add it to the `/etc/fstab` file."
