### 1. Video Script 7.1: Quantitative vs. Qualitative Risk
**Audio:** "Risk Management is how businesses decide how much money to spend on security. Qualitative risk is subjective: High, Medium, Low. It's fast, using a simple risk matrix. Quantitative risk uses hard dollar amounts. We calculate the Single Loss Expectancy (SLE)—how much a single server crash costs—and multiply it by the Annualized Rate of Occurrence (ARO) to find the Annualized Loss Expectancy (ALE). If the ALE is $10,000, we shouldn't spend $50,000 on a firewall."
