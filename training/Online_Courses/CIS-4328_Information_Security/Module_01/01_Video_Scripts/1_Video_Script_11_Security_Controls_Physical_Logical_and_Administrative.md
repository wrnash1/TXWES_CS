### 1. Video Script 1.1: Security Controls: Physical, Logical, and Administrative
**Estimated Duration:** 6 minutes
**Visual:** Instructor on camera.
**Audio:** "Welcome to Information Systems Security. To secure any environment, we apply 'Controls'. The CompTIA Security+ exam heavily tests your ability to classify a control into one of three main categories: Physical, Logical (or Technical), and Administrative (or Managerial)."
**Visual:** A three-column chart. Column 1: Physical (Fences, Cameras). Column 2: Logical (Firewalls, Passwords). Column 3: Administrative (Policies, Training).
**[Alt-text: A three-column table. Physical Controls list 'Fences, CCTV, Security Guards'. Logical Controls list 'Firewalls, Encryption, Passwords'. Administrative Controls list 'Acceptable Use Policies, Security Awareness Training, Audits'.]**
**Audio:** "Physical controls are things you can touch. A fence, a lock, a guard. Logical or Technical controls use technology to enforce security—like a firewall blocking traffic or an encryption algorithm. Administrative or Managerial controls are the rules of the game: your company's written policies, annual security training, and compliance audits."
