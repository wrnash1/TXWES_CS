### 2. Video Script 3.2: Cryptography: Symmetric vs Asymmetric Encryption
**Estimated Duration:** 7 minutes
**Visual:** Split screen comparing two encryption methods using lock-and-key analogies.
**[Alt-text: Left side labeled 'Symmetric': Alice and Bob both hold identical yellow keys to open a locked box. Right side labeled 'Asymmetric': Alice holds a red 'Public Key' padlocking a box. Bob holds a blue 'Private Key' unlocking it.]**
**Audio:** "Cryptography is the science of hiding data. The Security+ exam requires you to know two main types of encryption.
Symmetric encryption uses the *exact same key* to lock and unlock the data. It is very fast, making it great for encrypting entire hard drives (like BitLocker or AES). But, how do you securely share that single key across the internet?
That's where Asymmetric encryption comes in. It uses a *mathmatically linked key pair*. You give your Public Key to everyone in the world. Anyone can use it to encrypt a message to you. But only your Private Key—which you never share—can decrypt it. This is how SSL/TLS works to secure websites (using RSA or ECC)."
