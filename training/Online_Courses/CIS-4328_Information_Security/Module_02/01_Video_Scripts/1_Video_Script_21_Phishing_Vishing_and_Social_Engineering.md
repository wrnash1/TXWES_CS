### 1. Video Script 2.1: Phishing, Vishing, and Social Engineering
**Estimated Duration:** 6 minutes
**Visual:** Instructor on camera.
**Audio:** "Welcome to Module 2. The strongest firewalls and the most complex encryption algorithms are useless if an attacker can simply ask an employee for their password. This is Social Engineering: manipulating human psychology to bypass technical controls."
**Visual:** A bulleted list appears: Phishing (Email), Vishing (Voice/Phone), Smishing (SMS/Text), Spear Phishing (Targeted), Whaling (Targeting Executives).
**[Alt-text: A list defining social engineering vectors. Phishing = Email. Vishing = Voice. Smishing = SMS. Spear Phishing = Targeted individual. Whaling = Targeted high-level executive (CEO/CFO).]**
**Audio:** "The most common vector is Phishing, where an attacker sends a fraudulent email disguised as a trusted entity. If they target a specific individual, it's Spear Phishing. If they target the CEO or CFO, it's Whaling. We also see Vishing, where attackers call victims pretending to be IT support, and Smishing, which uses text messages. The mitigation for all of these is Administrative: rigorous, frequent Security Awareness Training."
