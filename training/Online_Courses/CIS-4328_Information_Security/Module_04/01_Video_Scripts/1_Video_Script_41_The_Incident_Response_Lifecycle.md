### 1. Video Script 4.1: The Incident Response Lifecycle
**Estimated Duration:** 6 minutes
**Visual:** Instructor on camera.
**Audio:** "Welcome to Module 4. No matter how good your architecture is, a breach will eventually happen. When it does, you don't panic; you follow the Incident Response Lifecycle. The Security+ exam breaks this down into strict phases."
**Visual:** A circular diagram showing the phases: 1. Preparation, 2. Identification, 3. Containment, 4. Eradication, 5. Recovery, 6. Lessons Learned.
**[Alt-text: A 6-step circular lifecycle diagram. 1: Preparation. 2: Identification. 3: Containment. 4: Eradication. 5: Recovery. 6: Lessons Learned.]**
**Audio:** "Preparation happens before the attack—writing policies and training staff. Identification is when you realize you've been breached. Containment is critical: do you unplug the infected server from the network to stop the spread, or leave it running to catch the hacker? Eradication is wiping the malware. Recovery is restoring from backups. Finally, Lessons Learned is the post-incident meeting where you figure out how to stop it from happening again."
