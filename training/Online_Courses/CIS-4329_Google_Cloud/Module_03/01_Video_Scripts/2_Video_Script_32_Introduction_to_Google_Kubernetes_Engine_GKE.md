### 2. Video Script 3.2: Introduction to Google Kubernetes Engine (GKE)
**Estimated Duration:** 7 minutes
**Visual:** A diagram comparing Virtual Machines (hypervisor) to Containers (Docker engine).
**[Alt-text: Left side: Hardware -> Hypervisor -> Three VMs, each with a full Guest OS. Right side: Hardware -> Host OS -> Container Engine (Docker) -> Three lightweight containers sharing the OS kernel.]**
**Audio:** "If VMs are entire virtual computers, Containers are just isolated virtual applications. Containers share the host's operating system, making them incredibly lightweight and fast to boot. However, if you have 5,000 containers running across 100 servers, how do you manage them? You use Kubernetes. Google Kubernetes Engine, or GKE, is the managed service where Google handles the Kubernetes control plane for you. You just deploy your containerized applications into 'Pods', and GKE ensures they stay running, load balances traffic, and scales them up or down."
